Digital Preservation:
The Vanishing Programmers (1980s)


Old Computer

Early Filipino programming contributions were poorly documented due to lack of archiving, limited media coverage, and political/economic challenges in the 1980s.

This old machines symbolize the roots of local innovation, showing how Filipinos were already engaging with computing despite limited resources.

Programmers

Gaps in archives affect our tech memory by erasing the achievements of pioneers, making it harder for future generations to recognize local innovators.

Many Filipino programmers contributed silently in the background, but their names and works often went unrecognized because documentation was incomplete.

Digitization

Digitization ensures these fading stories and innovations are preserved for future researchers, students, and innovators in the digital age.

Through scanning, oral histories, and archiving, we can rebuild the lost narratives of Filipino programmers and give credit where it is due.
